## Purpose

This document defines the creative and production assumptions required to keep the short film near a total production budget of JPY 10,000,000.

## Target Format

- Runtime: 18–20 minutes
- Format: 2K digital master
- Frame rate: 24 fps
- Primary style: 2D animation with limited 3D assistance
- Estimated cuts: 180–230
- Dialogue cast: 4–6 principal voices
- Main locations: 5

## Creative Constraints

- Keep the story centered on Harukawa Yo, Kura, Shiori and Koomote
- Do not depict the full Noumen organization
- Reuse the rainy shopping street, classroom and Emotion Bank backgrounds
- Keep crowd animation limited and concentrate detail on emotional close-ups
- Use compositing, lighting, rain, reflections and sound design to create production value
- Reserve the highest animation density for the emotional outbreak and Loved Gun climax

## Risk Notes

- A 20-minute film with TV-series-level full animation is not realistic at this budget
- Excessive character action, crowd scenes and new locations will create immediate overruns
- Voice talent, original music and studio rates can vary significantly
- A 10–15% contingency should be protected until final delivery
- The screenplay and storyboard must be cost-reviewed before production begins

## Cost-Control Priorities

1. Strong script and storyboard
2. Distinctive art direction
3. High-quality close-up acting
4. Rain, sound and lighting as emotional production value
5. Selective animation peaks rather than uniform density
