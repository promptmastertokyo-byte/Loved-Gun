# LOVED GUN — Lens Policy

Version 1.0  
Status: **Approved / authoritative for layout production**

## Purpose

This document fixes the production lens language for the 30 A-rank master layouts and the later B/C-rank expansion.

The goal is not to test every cut with every lens. Choose one primary lens language per scene, then change only when the story clearly requires it.

## Default Production Lenses

### 35mm — World

Use for:

- city and architecture
- establishing geography
- crowds
- isolation inside space
- returning the viewer to the world after an emotional close-up

### 50mm — Relationship / Default

Use for:

- dialogue
- two-shots
- character and environment in the same frame
- quiet psychological tension
- master layouts that must remain theatrical without losing readable acting

50mm is the default production lens for LOVED GUN.

### 85mm — Emotion / Detail

Use for:

- photographs
- hands
- eyes
- tears
- the bookmark
- memory fragments
- decisive emotional reactions

85mm must be used as emphasis, not as the default for an entire scene.

## Scene-Based Rule

1. Decide a primary lens for each scene.
2. Do not generate 35mm / 50mm / 85mm alternatives for every cut.
3. Change lens only when the visual subject changes in meaning.
4. A typical rhythm is:

```text
World / relationship (35mm or 50mm)
↓
Emotional detail (85mm)
↓
Return to relationship or world (50mm or 35mm)
```

## Wide-Angle Restriction

24mm and 28mm are not part of the normal production set.

They may be used only when all of the following are true:

- spatial distortion is essential to the story
- the effect cannot be achieved with staging, sound or performance
- the director explicitly approves the exception

Existing 24mm / 28mm notes in older storyboard files are historical planning notes and do not automatically control the A-rank master layouts.

## Scene 03 — Emotion Bank

Primary lens: **50mm**

- entrance and first meeting: 50mm
- Yo and Kura relationship / counter two-shot: 50mm
- photograph retrieval and handoff: 85mm
- photograph itself: 85mm or macro insert
- Kura's reaction to the photograph: 85mm
- contract decision and return to the two-shot: 50mm

The scene should move from space → relationship → photograph → relationship.

## Scene 10 — Emotional Collapse

Primary lens: **50mm theatrical wide**

- frozen city and Yo together: 50mm
- Yo and delayed shadow readable in the same frame: 50mm
- shadow / hand / particle detail: 85mm
- emotional face detail: 85mm
- return to the frozen world: 50mm

Follow `SCENE10_LAYOUT_OVERRIDE.md` for camera timing and staging.

## Production Check

Before approving a layout, confirm:

- the lens has a storytelling reason
- the shot is not wide merely to look cinematic
- the shot is not close merely to make emotion obvious
- wide, medium and close framing create rhythm across the scene
- the approved composition can be preserved by Seedance without reframing
