# LOVED GUN — Scene 10 Layout Override

Version 1.1  
Status: **Approved / authoritative for layout production**

This document resolves the earlier wide-angle lens guidance in `STORYBOARD_BIBLE.md` and `scene10.md`.
For Scene 10 master layouts, this file, `LENS_POLICY.md` and `MASTER_A_LAYOUT_GUIDE.md` take precedence.

## Approved Lens Structure

### Master / Frozen World

- **Lens language:** 50mm-equivalent theatrical wide composition
- **Framing:** environment-first wide shot; Yo and his shadow remain readable together
- **Character scale:** Yo occupies roughly 25–30% of frame height
- **Environment:** station plaza, frozen crowd, rain reflections and architecture remain visible
- **Tone:** psychological drama and quiet abnormality, not action or monster horror

### Emotional Details

Use 85mm only for deliberate emphasis:

- the delayed shadow
- Yo's hand or photograph
- black particles leaking from the shadow
- Yo's eyes or restrained distress

Return to 50mm after the detail so the frozen world remains part of the scene.

24mm and 28mm are not the A-rank production standard for this scene. They remain historical storyboard notes unless the director explicitly approves a distortion shot.

## Camera Direction

1. Camera slowly trucks from right to left until Yo enters the approved composition.
2. Camera comes to a complete stop.
3. Hold the frame in near silence.
4. Yo remains still except for breathing.
5. Only the shadow moves half a beat later.
6. The audience should notice the shadow before Yo does.

Do not combine camera motion and shadow motion at the same time.
The shadow event begins only after the camera has fully stopped.

## Visual Rhythm

```text
50mm frozen city / Yo
↓
85mm shadow or hand detail
↓
50mm Yo + shadow + frozen crowd
↓
85mm emotional or particle detail
↓
50mm return to the world
```

The scene must not remain wide for every cut. The master shot establishes the world; detail shots reveal the internal collapse.

## Seedance Lock

- Preserve the approved layout, perspective and crop.
- Do not enlarge Yo into TV-anime close framing.
- Do not orbit, zoom, reframe or redesign the station plaza.
- Animate only the slow truck, rain, breathing and delayed shadow in sequence.
- Crowd remains completely frozen.
- Black particles remain one restrained effect stream.
- Use 85mm details only where the storyboard assigns a specific emotional subject.
