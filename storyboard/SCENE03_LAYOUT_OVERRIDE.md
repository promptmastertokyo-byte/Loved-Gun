# LOVED GUN — Scene 03 Layout Override

Version 1.0  
Status: **Approved / authoritative for A-rank layout production**

This document resolves the older 28mm / 35mm notes in `scene03.md` for the approved master-layout phase.

## Dramatic Function

Scene 03 introduces both the Emotion Bank and the relationship between Yo and Kura.

The layout rhythm is:

```text
Space
↓
Relationship
↓
Photograph
↓
Relationship
```

The scene must not remain wide throughout, and it must not become a sequence of TV-anime face close-ups.

## Approved Lens Structure

### Entrance / First Meeting

- **Lens:** 50mm
- Show the Emotion Bank interior, Yo and Kura in one coherent space.
- Preserve architectural depth, windows, counter and negative space.
- The first meeting is a relationship shot, not merely an establishing shot.

### Counter Dialogue

- **Lens:** 50mm
- Use two-shots and restrained medium framing.
- Keep an object between Yo and Kura: magazine, terminal, photograph or Loved Gun.
- Preserve emotional distance through staging rather than excessive wide angle.

### Photograph Retrieval and Handoff

- **Lens:** 85mm
- Photograph leaving Yo's pocket.
- Fingers tightening around the folded photograph.
- Photograph crossing the counter.
- Kura's hand receiving it.
- Kura's restrained reaction.

The photograph is the narrative trigger and should receive the strongest visual emphasis in the scene.

### Photograph Insert

- **Lens:** 85mm or macro-equivalent insert
- Show fold, rain stain and the obscured face clearly.
- Do not add decorative shallow-depth effects that reduce story readability.

### Contract Decision / Return

- **Lens:** 50mm
- Return to Yo and Kura in the same frame.
- Re-establish the distance between them after the photograph inserts.
- The audience should feel that the relationship has changed even though the physical space is unchanged.

## Framing Rhythm

Recommended sequence:

```text
50mm interior / first meeting
↓
50mm two-shot
↓
85mm photograph and hands
↓
85mm reaction
↓
50mm two-shot / contract
```

## Seedance Lock

- Preserve approved camera position, architecture and character placement.
- Do not convert 50mm relationship shots into character-dominant close framing.
- Do not keep the whole scene in a wide master shot.
- Animate only the specified acting element in each cut.
- No orbit, automatic push-in, reframing or background redesign.
